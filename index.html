campus life

Grand Staircase

Rowena Ravenclaw was the Hogwarts founder who invented the concept of moving stairs.

The Grand Staircase is a massive stairway in Hogwarts Castle, the structure was mainly used to access each floor of the castle, including the dungeons.

Grand Hall

There is a throne-like chair in the centre of the table where the current Headmaster or Headmistress sits.

The Great Hall is a large hall with four long tables where students of each House sit at during mealtimes, as well as the staff table at the front, where the entire Hogwarts staff sits.

quidditch

Quidditch, “the sport of warlocks,” is the premier sport of the wizarding world. Everyone follows Quidditch.

Quidditch is a exciting game in which two teams flying on brooms compete for points scored by throwing a ball – the Quaffle – through hoops on either end of a large grassy pitch. Quidditch is played by children on broomsticks in the back apple orchard, by teams of students at Hogwarts and by professional athletes whose exploits are followed avidly all over the world.

Hogwarts get Rebuilt

The original Hogwarts was built through magical means and very quickly rebuilds itself whenever it becomes damaged. It also adds new spaces and facilities as the professors and students require them. Hogwarts actually exists in a separate dimension distinct from the reality of the outer World.
